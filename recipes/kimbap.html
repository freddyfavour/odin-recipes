Ingredients

  • 2 cups short-grain sushi rice
  • 5 sheets roasted seaweed (nori)
  • 1 carrot, julienned
  • 1 cucumber, julienned
  • 1/2 pound (225g) imitation crab sticks or cooked seasoned beef/ham, thinly sliced
  • 5 strips yellow pickled radish (danmuji)
  • 5 eggs, beaten and cooked into thin omelets, then sliced into strips
  • Sesame oil for brushing

Steps

  1. Rinse sushi rice until water runs clear. Cook rice and, while warm, mix with rice vinegar, sugar, and salt. Let it cool.
  2. Place a bamboo sushi rolling mat on a flat surface, covered with plastic wrap. Lay nori on top, shiny side down.
  3. Wet your hands, spread a thin layer of rice over nori, leaving 1 inch at the top.
  4. Horizontally arrange carrot, cucumber, crab/ham, pickled radish, and egg strips along the bottom edge of the rice.
  5. Roll tightly from the bottom, using the bamboo mat. Seal the edge with a bit of water.
  6. Brush rolled Gimbap with sesame oil for flavor and to prevent nori from becoming too chewy.
  7. Let it sit for a few minutes to seal, then slice into bite-sized pieces.
  8. Serve with soy sauce or your favorite dipping sauce. Your Gimbap adventure begins!
