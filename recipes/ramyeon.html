Ingredients

  • 2 packs of Shin Ramyun (or your favorite Korean ramyeon)
  • 4 cups water
  • 1 cup Napa cabbage, chopped
  • 1/2 cup mushrooms, sliced
  • 1/2 cup carrots, julienned
  • 1/2 cup green onions, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 2 eggs (optional)
  • Sesame seeds and nori (seaweed) for garnish

Steps

  1. Heat vegetable oil in a pot over medium heat. Add minced garlic and sautÃ© until fragrant.
  2. Add the Napa cabbage, mushrooms, and carrots to the pot. Stir-fry for a few minutes until the vegetables start to soften.
  3. Pour in the water and bring it to a boil.
  4. Once the water is boiling, add the ramyeon noodles and seasoning packets from the Shin Ramyun packs. If you prefer less spice, you can adjust the amount of the spicy seasoning.
  5. Cook the noodles according to the package instructions, usually for about 4-5 minutes.
  6. If you like, you can crack eggs into the pot during the last 2 minutes of cooking. Allow them to poach in the broth.
  7. Add chopped green onions and stir.
  8. Once the noodles are cooked, remove the pot from heat.
  9. Serve the ramyeon in bowls, making sure to distribute the vegetables and broth evenly.
  10. Garnish with sesame seeds and torn nori sheets.

Enjoy your Korean ramyeon! Feel free to add or modify the ingredients to suit your taste. Some people like to add kimchi, tofu, or even a slice of cheese for extra richness.
